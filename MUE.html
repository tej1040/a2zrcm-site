What Is MUE?

MUE stands for Medically Unlikely Edit. It is the maximum number of units Medicare will pay for a CPT/HCPCS code for a single patient on a single date of service. MUEs prevent:

  • Billing typos (like adding an extra zero)
  • Medically impossible units
  • Fraud or abusive billing
  • Duplicate billing of the same service

Example: If the MUE for a code is 1 and a provider bills 3 units, Medicare will pay only 1 and deny the rest as ‚ÄúUnits exceed MUE.‚Äù

Why Does Medicare Use MUE?

  • To enforce medically reasonable limits
  • To prevent incorrect billing patterns
  • To ensure national claim consistency
  • To reduce unnecessary appeals and reviews
  • To stop system errors and duplicate lines

For coders and AR teams, understanding MUEs helps prevent denials and ensures claims are billed with correct unit structure.

What Is MAI?

MAI stands for MUE Adjudication Indicator. MAI tells Medicare how strictly an MUE is enforced and whether an appeal is possible.

There are three MAI categories used for MUE edits:

  • MAI 1 ‚Äì Claim-level edit (appeals may be possible)
  • MAI 2 ‚Äì Absolute edit (no appeals allowed)
  • MAI 3 ‚Äì Clinical edit (appeals allowed only in rare cases)
